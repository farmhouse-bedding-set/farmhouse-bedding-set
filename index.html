WASH BEDDING PERIODICALLY

Washing bedding is one of the most time-consuming household chores. It's no surprise that many of us tend to leave it on a little longer, especially when our linens "do not look dirty". But just because you can't see the dust particles and bacteria, doesn't mean they aren't there. And chances are you don't make your bed as often as you should.

Experts say you should wash all your bed sheets, duvet covers, pillowcases, and mattress covers once a week. This may sound like a lot, but we all know how quickly bacteria can grow and multiply in unwashed sheets. There are a lot of germs and bacteria that accumulate in a short period of time. Bacterial buildup in bed is largely due to sweat, body oils, and dead cells (which attract dust mites) that stick from your body onto the sheets while you sleep.

The study found that the distribution of bacteria on pillowcases was slightly lower than on bed sheets, but not by much. We tend to wash our faces more often than our bodies, but products like moisturizers and other nighttime skin care products also build up quickly on pillowcases. This encourages the growth of germs right on the pillow.

In fact, you may find that changing your pillowcase more than once a week helps control oily skin and breakouts. So if you have acne, you should change your pillowcase regularly to limit its growth.

According to the recommendations of medical experts on health care, we should clean bedding periodically every 2-3 months. However, during the covid season, you should change and wash them more than twice a month.

This helps prevent germs that can breed in bed sheets, pillowcases, and duvet covers, as well as preventing the accumulation of house dust mites, their droppings, and the dead skin they feed on. In fact, a bedroom is an ideal place for dust mites to breed and thrive. Beds, carpets, and mattresses all trapped moisture, allowing these little bugs to breed.

Wash sheets, pillowcases, blankets, and mattress covers in hot water at least once a week. Dry them in natural sunlight or machine dry them.

If you are a person with sensitive skin, the more you need to keep your bed clean. On average, we lie in bed for 7-8 hours a day, so our body is in direct contact with blankets and sheets. Dirty sheets help bacteria and dust mites accumulate for a long time, causing allergy symptoms.

When we are allergic to dust mites, we often mistakenly think that we are allergic to weather, so we should buy medicine to drink. Dust mite allergies in people with asthma often cause severe symptoms such as shortness of breath, shortness of breath, chest pain, and severe asthma attacks. If you're prone to allergies or asthma, it's best to change your bed sheets more than once a week, to minimize dust and dust mites.

Therefore, regular cleaning of beddings and pillows is extremely important, not only to help keep the room clean and beautiful but also to maximize health. Even so, a survey by the Sleep Council found that about a quarter of us tend to change our sheets only once a month.
